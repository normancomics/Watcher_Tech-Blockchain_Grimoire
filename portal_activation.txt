2. Smart Contracts & Encryption Origins

  

  

Smart Contracts:

  

- Concept first formalized by Nick Szabo in the 1990s.
- Implemented on blockchains (like Ethereum) to enforce conditional agreements automatically.

  

  

Encryption:

  

- SHA-256: Developed by the NSA (part of the SHA-2 family, published 2001) for cryptographic hashing.
- AES-256: Developed by Joan Daemen & Vincent Rijmen (published 2001, as Rijndael) for symmetric-key encryption.
- Fully Homomorphic & Quantum-Resistant Cryptography: Modern cryptographers and research institutions (MIT, IBM, Microsoft, and post-quantum research consortia) are developing these; public domain but theoretically influenced by advanced mathematics and quantum physics.
